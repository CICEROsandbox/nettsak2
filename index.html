The Rise of Global CO₂ Emissions

Over the past three decades, global carbon dioxide emissions have shown a persistent upward trend.

Since 1990, we've witnessed an unprecedented increase in global CO₂ emissions from fossil fuels.

Early 1990s Decline

The dissolution of the Soviet Union led to a temporary decrease in global emissions.

The Great Recession

The 2008-2009 financial crisis caused the first significant drop in emissions.

Looking Ahead

The challenge of reducing emissions while maintaining economic growth becomes increasingly critical.
